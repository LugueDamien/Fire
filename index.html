How to prevent fires in your house

Welcome to our fire prevention website, dedicated to providing essential information and resources to help you prevent fires and protect yourself and your property. Our mission is to raise awareness about fire safety and educate individuals and communities on how to minimize the risk of fires. Fires can cause significant damage and result in devastating losses, including property damage, injuries, and even loss of life. However, with the right knowledge and precautions, fires can be prevented, and their impact can be minimized.

Install smoke detectors

Make sure to have smoke detectors installed on every level of your home, including in each bedroom and near the kitchen. Test them regularly to ensure they are working correctly.

Avoid overloading electrical outlets

Do not overload electrical outlets or use extension cords in place of permanent wiring. This can cause electrical fires.

Keep flammable objects away from heat sources

Keep flammable objects, such as curtains, towels, and papers, away from heat sources like stovetops, fireplaces, and space heaters.

Turn off appliances when not in use

Turn off and unplug appliances when not in use, especially before going to bed or leaving the house.

Keep a fire extinguisher

Keep a fire extinguisher in your home, and make sure everyone in the house knows how to use it.
